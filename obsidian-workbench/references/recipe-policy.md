# Recipe Policy

Use this reference when the note may create, revise, or link a `Recipe`.

## What Counts As A Recipe
- A Recipe owns a strong reusable procedure.
- A future reader should be able to reuse it next time with stable steps, evidence shape, permitted conclusion, forbidden conclusion, and boundary.
- The procedure is owned by the method, not by one case.

## What Must Not Become A Recipe
- A caution
- A weakening result
- A proof boundary
- A partial contributor
- A case-shaped observation
- Any branch that does not yet own stable next-time steps

Keep those in the story, checklist, Deep note, or raw issue record instead of promoting them into `Recipes/`.

## Minimum Recipe Contract
- First line: `#Recipe`
- Use case near the start: say when the reader should reach for it
- Clear step sequence
- Evidence shape and boundary where that matters
- No bottom `## Related` section
- Keep the `Recipe` prefix visible when linking

## Link Discipline
- Search existing `Recipes/` first.
- If one Recipe already owns the branch, link to it instead of cloning its steps.
- Avoid duplicate Recipe links unless the local checkpoint or paragraph truly needs direct landing on that exact Recipe.
